    AI + Social Science Workshop

    Leo Y. Yang
    Event notice
    School of Economics and Management, Wuhan University

    Abstract

    Shared the talk “Predictive Power, Bias, and Trust: Evaluating LLM-Driven Screening in FinTech Lending” during the methodological training sessions that focused on how large language models complement traditional empirical work.

    Date
    Nov 4, 2025 8:30 AM — 4:00 PM
    Event
    Luojia EMS Frontier Methods Bootcamp X & AI + Social Science Workshop
    Location
    Wuhan University School of Economics and Management
    299 Bayi Road, Luojia Hill, Wuhan, Hubei 430072

    I joined the tenth Luojia EMS Frontier Methods Bootcamp & AI + Social Science Workshop to exchange ideas on how generative AI reshapes empirical research in the social sciences. Hosted by the School of Economics and Management at Wuhan University, the workshop convened Raymond Duch, Lijia Wei, Lian Xue, Peiran Jiao, and other colleagues for a full day of methodological training and research dialogue.

    Session highlights

    • 08:30-10:00 - Frontier methods overview. Raymond Duch outlined how LLMs can act as experimental subjects, pollsters, and market analysts, followed by Lijia Wei’s evidence on the Matching-LLM framework for forecasting public opinion.
    • 10:20-11:50 - Research methods training. Lian Xue discussed belief updating with medical AI explanations, and Peiran Jiao linked employee voice data from Glassdoor to firm valuation models.
    • 11:50-12:35 - My presentation. I presented Predictive Power, Bias, and Trust: Evaluating LLM-Driven Screening in FinTech Lending, showing how model governance and human oversight mitigate screening bias.
    • 14:00-16:00 - Roundtable discussion. Speakers stayed on for a closed-door session to trade implementation lessons on building trustworthy AI workflows for social-science data.

    These conversations fed directly into my ongoing AIECON collaborations on data-centric AI, regulation, and human-centered evaluation of large language models.
