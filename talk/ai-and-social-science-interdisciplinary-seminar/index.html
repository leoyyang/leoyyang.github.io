Invited by Xi Weng at the Guanghua School of Management, Peking University, I presented at the “AI and Social Science” interdisciplinary seminar series.

Talk: Scaling Reproducibility

Computational reproducibility is central to scientific credibility, yet verifying published results at scale remains costly. I introduced an AI-assisted workflow for automated full-paper replication — retrieving materials, reconstructing environments, executing code, and matching outputs to point estimates reported in regression tables.

Key findings from the study:

  • Universe definition. We define a universe of all empirical and quantitative papers from the three top political science journals (2010–2025) and measure stated data availability using automated extraction.
  • Replication at scale. For a stratified sample of 384 studies, we apply the workflow to conduct full-paper replication, totaling 3,382 empirical models.
  • Impact of mandates. Journal verification requirements, combined with data archiving mandates, drive reproducibility: the full-paper reproducibility rate rises from 29.6% before DA-RT adoption to 79.8% after, and conditional on accessible replication packages, 94.4% of papers are fully reproducible (237/251).
  • IV diagnostics. As a secondary application, we apply standardized IV diagnostics to 92 studies (215 specifications), illustrating how automated execution enables systematic reanalysis across heterogeneous empirical settings.
