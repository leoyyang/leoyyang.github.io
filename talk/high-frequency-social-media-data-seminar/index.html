    High-Frequency Social Media Data Seminar

    Leo Y. Yang
    Event recap

    Abstract

    Presented the talk “High-Frequency Social Media Data: User Location Labels and Their Impact on Online Communities,” showing how Weibo’s 2022 IP-tag policy can be used to study identity construction, moderation, and bias across public affairs discussions.

    Date
    Oct 22, 2025 12:00 AM — 11:59 PM
    Event
    Social Survey & Application Lecture Series
    Location
    Jinan University Social Survey Center
    601 Huangpu Avenue West, Guangzhou, Guangdong 510632

    Invited by the Jinan University Social Survey Center, I delivered Lecture 55 in the “Social Survey & Application” series on the policy shock created by Weibo’s April 2022 IP disclosure requirement. The project tracks 200 news accounts and the top comment chains around each post to observe how geography-linked identity cues steer civic debate.

    Research overview

    • Data engineering. I detailed the pipeline we built to capture pre/post policy posts, scrape reply trees, and annotate user locations while protecting user privacy.
    • Behavioral impact. Comparing domestic and overseas commenters reveals divergent participation patterns once location tags become visible, alongside a measurable drop in hostile replies.
    • Implications for moderators. Location transparency changes how online communities interpret credibility and allegiances, so we discussed how to pair machine flags with human review to keep deliberation open while curbing abuse.

    We closed with a Q&A on fielding API-rate constraints, validating location cues, and extending the design to other high-frequency social platforms.
